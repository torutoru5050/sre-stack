## TL;DR

**Notion AI** excels at writing, summarization, and knowledge management. **ClickUp AI** is stronger for project management workflows — task generation, standups, and sprint planning. Choose based on whether your team lives in docs or in tasks.

## AI Writing & Editing

Notion was a writing tool first, and their AI reflects that. Notion AI can draft documents, summarize pages, translate content, and rewrite text with different tones. It feels natural — like having an editor built into every page.

ClickUp AI also generates and edits text, but it's more template-driven. It shines when creating task descriptions, user stories, and meeting notes from predefined formats. Less freeform, more structured.

| Feature | Notion AI | ClickUp AI |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Freeform writing | Excellent | Good |
| Summarization | Excellent | Good |
| Task generation | Basic | Excellent |
| Standup reports | Manual | Automated |
| Translation | Yes | Yes |
| Custom AI fields | No | Yes |

## Project Management

ClickUp is purpose-built for project management, and their AI features reflect that. Auto-generated subtasks, sprint point estimation, standup summaries from task activity, and AI-powered project templates save real time.

Notion can do project management with databases, but it's more DIY. You build your own system with templates and relations. Notion AI helps _within_ that system (summarize a project page, draft a PRD) but doesn't automate the PM workflow itself.

## Knowledge Management

This is Notion's home turf. Notion AI can answer questions about your workspace — "What did the team decide about the API migration?" — by searching across all your pages. It's like having a team wiki with built-in search that actually understands context.

ClickUp has docs and wikis too, but they're secondary to the task management core. The AI search across ClickUp docs works but isn't as refined as Notion's Q&A experience.

## Integrations

Both tools integrate with the usual suspects (Slack, GitHub, Google Workspace), but the depth differs:

- **Notion** has a powerful API and a rich ecosystem of community integrations. It's become a hub for team knowledge.
- **ClickUp** integrates deeper with development tools — GitHub PRs linked to tasks, time tracking, Gantt charts, and native git integration.

## Pricing

- **Notion Free**: Unlimited pages, 7-day page history
- **Notion Plus**: $10/user/month — unlimited file uploads, 30-day history
- **Notion AI Add-on**: $10/user/month (on top of any plan)
- **ClickUp Free**: Unlimited tasks, 100 MB storage
- **ClickUp Unlimited**: $7/user/month — unlimited storage, integrations
- **ClickUp AI**: Included in all paid plans

ClickUp's AI inclusion in paid plans is a significant advantage. With Notion, AI is always an add-on — a team of 10 pays an extra $100/month just for AI features.

## When to Choose What

- **Choose Notion AI** if your team is docs-heavy, you need a powerful knowledge base, or you want the best AI writing experience in a workspace tool.
- **Choose ClickUp AI** if your team is task-heavy, you need AI-automated project management workflows, or you want AI included without extra cost.

## Bottom Line

The Notion vs ClickUp decision usually comes down to team culture. Document-first teams love Notion. Task-first teams love ClickUp. The AI features amplify whichever workflow you already prefer. If you're starting fresh, ClickUp's included AI pricing makes it the better value. If you already have a Notion workspace full of docs, the AI add-on is worth it for the knowledge Q&A alone.
